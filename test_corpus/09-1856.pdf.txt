MITRE CMMI-DEV and CMMI-ACQ Version 1.2 Appraisal and Reporting Tools
Have you ever gone into an audit, appraisal, or assessment of an organizations capability
without a single tool to help organize and manage all the information, data, and results? Ive used
spreadsheets, hand-written notes, and Word documents to collect and log information and data,
and later had to manipulate that information and data to produce reports. That process of
evaluating the results and developing reports would take substantial time to complete. I wanted
something to help reduce the time and effort to pull all the information and data together and
report on the results. In addition, I wanted something that could help guide and be easily used by
an organization for performing their own self-assessment of their strengths and weaknesses for
preparing to go through a CMMI appraisal.

Developed over four years ago, my first versions of these tools were used on SCAMPI C and
SCAMPI A appraisals. Although the first versions were useful, I knew I could strengthen their
content and usability. For example, having multiple spreadsheets for each process area report
was awkward. I knew I could improve the tools and offer them across MITRE for process
improvement projects.

I got my chance when I saw that Lynda Rosa had included a section on process improvement in
the MITRE Monday email. I contacted Lynda who got the ball rolling. Lynda and John Kennedy
reviewed the earlier version of the tools and provided some suggestions for improvement. Lynda
put me in touch with the Systems Engineering Process Office (SEPO) for possible sponsorship.
With SEPO support and sponsorship, I was able to dedicate time to enhance and update the tools
to the CMMI Version 1.2 for Development and Acquisition.

The tools can be used as a supporting mechanism for accumulating and managing information
and data related to CMMI type appraisals. Their charts and reports outline maturity/capability
level and associated risk ratings. The tools include:


A single spreadsheet master workbook for all 22 process areas of either the DEV or ACQ
modules for gathering data and information, including
o Use and Priority spreadsheets that provide traffic light coloring for
maturity/capability and risk level ratings for all process areas for levels 25.

o Process Area spreadsheets that provide descriptions of the specific and generic
practices for maturity/capability levels 25 as well as columns and rows for
scoring and logging artifact information, data, and comments.
o Instructions.


Supporting workbooks that use the data from this master workbook for producing
graphical reports for maturity/capability levels 05.
